Get a Clearer Picture of Emissions

Vehicle emissions are a burden, both to the Earth and our health. Civic administrators, environmental bureaus, and law enforcement agencies must find new ways to curtail vehicle emissions, without halting traffic or otherwise being disruptive. But when it comes to air pollution, it can be very hard to determine individually who is at fault – D-Link has solutions.

Vehicle-level
Compliance

Vehicles account for 15% of global emissions, and many cars and trucks on the road today, even newer ones, are non-compliant. D-Link offers solutions that can leverage video surveillance infrastructure already in place, or new infrastructure. Automated Number Plate Recognition (ANPR) technology can utilize video feeds from roadside cameras. If a tailpipe is observed producing black smoke, an automated alert can be raised, with that vehicle’s license plate identified – it’s that simple.

District-level Compliance

The temptation can be great for an individual district within a jurisdiction to be lax in its enforcement of emissions regulations. But D-Link has the analytics solutions needed to help stimulate compliance. Data from ambient environmental sensors can be analyzed in the context of other relevant information, such as wind patterns, to determine which districts are the sources of emissions.

District-level Compliance

Data-Driven Decisionmaking

D-Link facilitates data-driven decisionmaking through real-time ambient environmental analytics. For instance, if administrators at a national park repeatedly observe high emission levels within the park during weekends and holidays from visiting cars, they might decide to eliminate parking onsite and institute an electric shuttle bus service for bringing visitors into the park. And this is just one example. There are many other ways that environmental Big Data can be used to drive eco-friendly governance.
